His research explores the intersection of:




Finite deterministic dynamical systems


Arithmetic dynamics

AQARION Research develops computational frameworks that transform discrete mathematical processes into analyzable dynamical systems, allowing states, transitions, attractors, symmetries, and invariants to be studied through mathematical modeling and exact computation.

Finite Deterministic Dynamical Systems


The primary research program, AQARION-ARITHMETIC, studies finite deterministic systems:


[
T:X\rightarrow X
]


where each state has exactly one successor.


These systems naturally form functional graphs containing:




Attracting cycles


Transient states


Rooted tree structures


Basin geometries


Algebraic and spectral signatures




AQARION develops methods for converting these structures into mathematical objects suitable for classification and verification.

The well-known 4-digit Kaprekar system and its attractor 6174 provide a benchmark environment for testing finite dynamical methods.



Observable Quotient Research


AQARION investigates when a reduced representation of a dynamical system preserves the original system's observable behavior.


A central framework uses the defect operator:


[
D_{\Pi}=(I-P_{\Pi})KP_{\Pi}
]


where:




K represents the transition dynamics


P_{\Pi} represents an observable projection


D_{\Pi} measures dynamical leakage outside the proposed quotient space




The research explores the relationship between:




exact observable compression


invariant subspaces


quotient dynamics


operator behavior





The Commutator Fallacy


One investigated principle concerns the distinction between:


[
D_{\Pi}=0
]


and:


[
[P_{\Pi},K]=0
]


AQARION research examines why preservation of quotient dynamics does not necessarily require complete commutation between projection and transition operators.


This distinction provides a more precise framework for analyzing observable reductions in finite systems.



Fibre Geometry of Functional Graphs


A major research focus is the search for complete invariants of finite deterministic systems.


AQARION studies whether common descriptors are sufficient:




cycle structure


image filtration


depth distributions


branching statistics


spectral information




Experimental investigation indicates that transient fibre geometry contains structural information that many traditional invariants fail to capture.


The proposed functional graph signature:


[
S(T)=\left(C,{\tau(c)}\right)
]


combines:




cycle structure C


canonical rooted fibre trees \tau(c)




The goal is a complete structural description of finite functional graphs through minimal information.
