James A. Skaggs — Independent Researcher & Computational Mathematics Architect

Founder, AQARION Research

James A. Skaggs is an independent researcher and computational architect focused on the intersection of arithmetic dynamics, finite deterministic systems, graph theory, spectral analysis, and AI-assisted mathematical discovery.

Through the AQARION Research initiative, Skaggs develops computational frameworks for studying how simple mathematical transformations can generate complex structures, with particular emphasis on finite dynamical systems, invariant discovery, and observable quotient construction.

His research explores the transformation of classical arithmetic processes — including Kaprekar-style digit dynamics — into structured mathematical objects represented through functional graphs, algebraic systems, and spectral geometries. The central research philosophy is that discrete mathematical systems can reveal hidden organization when examined through the combined lenses of computation, graph structure, and rigorous verification.

AQARION-ARITHMETIC is a research program dedicated to the study of finite deterministic dynamical systems (FDDS), focusing on:

- Exact observable quotient certification
- Invariant discovery in discrete systems
- Functional graph analysis
- Spectral characterization of arithmetic dynamics
- Computational proof auditing and verification workflows

A major theme of the research is understanding when complex deterministic systems can be compressed into simpler observable representations while preserving essential dynamics. This work investigates the relationship between arithmetic transformations, attractor structures, graph geometry, and emergent mathematical organization.

Skaggs’ broader vision is the development of research environments where human mathematical intuition and computational intelligence work together to explore, test, and refine new mathematical structures.

Research Areas
