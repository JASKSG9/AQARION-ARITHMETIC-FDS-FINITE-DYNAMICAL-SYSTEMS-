3. Mathematical Core (Frozen)

3.1 Defect Operator

\boxed{D_\Pi = (I - P_\Pi) K P_\Pi} 

Symbol Meaning
$X$ Finite state space
$T: X \to X$ Deterministic transition
$\Pi$ Partition induced by observable $\pi$
$P_\Pi$ Orthogonal projection onto $\Pi$-constant functions
$K$ Koopman operator: $(Kf)(x) = f(T(x))$
$D_\Pi$ Measures "leakage" of information out of the observable subspace

3.2 Descent Criterion (T0)

\boxed{D_\Pi = 0 \iff K(V_\Pi) \subseteq V_\Pi \iff \Pi \text{ is a forward congruence}} 

Equivalences:

· $D_\Pi = 0$ → The partition is invariant under the dynamics
· $K(V_\Pi) \subseteq V_\Pi$ → Observable subspace is closed
· $\Pi$ is a forward congruence → $x \sim_\Pi y \implies T(x) \sim_\Pi T(y)$
· Exact quotient $\bar T$ exists with $\pi \circ T = \bar T \circ \pi$

Evidence: [P+CV+FV] — human proof, exhaustive computation, Lean 4 verification

3.3 Lattice Structure (T003)

\mathcal{E}(T) = \{\Pi : D_\Pi = 0\} 

Theorem: $\mathcal{E}(T)$ is a sublattice of the partition lattice $\text{Part}(X)$:

· Meet closure: $\Pi_1, \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T) \implies \Pi_1 \wedge \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T)$
· Join closure: $\Pi_1, \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T) \implies \Pi_1 \vee \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T)$

Not true: Distributivity or modularity in general (counterexamples at $n=3$ and $n=4$)

Evidence: [P+CV+FV] — proof + exhaustive verification + Lean skeleton

3.4 Escape Filtration (CLOSURE-001)

For any non-exact partition $\Pi_0$:

\Pi_{k+1} = \Pi_k \vee T^{-1}(\Pi_k), \qquad E_{k+1} = E_k + K(E_k) 

Limit: $\Pi_\infty =$ minimal exact refinement above $\Pi_0$, $E_\infty = V_{\Pi_\infty}$

Invariant Definition
$\delta_1$ $\operatorname{rank}(D_\Pi) = \dim(E_1) - \dim(E_0)$
$m(\Pi)$ $\dim(E_\infty) - \dim(E_0)$
$h(\Pi)$ $\min\{k : E_k = E_\infty\}$
$\Gamma_\Pi$ $(\delta_0, \delta_1, \ldots, \delta_\infty)$

Key inequality: $\operatorname{rank}(D_\Pi) \le m(\Pi)$, equality iff $h=1$

1. Introduction

1.1 Motivation

In many areas of science and engineering, we seek to reduce a complex dynamical system to a smaller "effective" system that preserves essential behavior. When the reduction is exact—i.e., when every trajectory of the original system projects to a trajectory of the reduced system—we say the system admits an exact quotient.

For finite deterministic systems $(X, T)$, an observable $\pi: X \to Y$ induces an exact quotient iff the fibers of $\pi$ are transition-congruent:

x \sim_\pi y \;\Longrightarrow\; T(x) \sim_\pi T(y) 

This condition is algebraic, but in practice it is often checked by exhaustive search or by heuristic invariants such as the commutator $[K, P_\Pi]$. We show that the commutator is not a reliable certificate.

AQARION introduces a defect operator that measures the failure of a partition to be a transition congruence:

D_\Pi = (I - P_\Pi) K P_\Pi 

When $D_\Pi = 0$, the partition is exact—the quotient exists. When $D_\Pi \neq 0$, its singular spectrum provides a geometric picture of the obstruction.

2. Mathematical Framework

2.1 Finite Deterministic Dynamical Systems

Let $X$ be a finite set, $|X| = n$, and let $T: X \to X$ be a deterministic map. The Koopman operator $K$ acts on observables $f: X \to \mathbb{R}$ by:

(K f)(x) = f(T(x)) 

In the standard basis, $K$ is the $n \times n$ matrix $K_{ij} = \delta_{T(j), i}$ (pullback convention).

2.2 Partitions and Projections

A partition $\Pi$ of $X$ into blocks $B_1, \dots, B_k$ defines a projection $P_\Pi$ onto the subspace of functions constant on blocks:

(P_\Pi f)(x) = \frac{1}{|B|} \sum_{y \in B} f(y), \qquad x \in B 

In matrix form, $P_\Pi \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$ is block-diagonal with entries $1/|B|$.

2.3 The Observable Subspace

The block-constant subspace is:

V_\Pi = \{ f : X \to \mathbb{R} \mid f \text{ is constant on each } B_i \} 

The projection $P_\Pi$ maps $\mathbb{R}^X$ onto $V_\Pi$.

3. The Defect Operator

3.1 Definition

\boxed{D_\Pi = (I - P_\Pi) K P_\Pi} 

Interpretation: $D_\Pi$ measures the component of the evolved observable that lies outside the subspace of block-constant functions. If it is zero, the evolution preserves the observable subspace—the definition of an exact quotient.

3.2 Structural Properties

Property Statement Evidence
Nilpotency $D_\Pi^2 = 0$ [P]
Rank Ceiling $\operatorname{rank}(D_\Pi) \le n - \Pi
Commutator Dominance $\operatorname{rank}(D_\Pi) \le \operatorname{rank}(C_\Pi)$ [P]
Exactness Criterion $D_\Pi = 0 \iff K(V_\Pi) \subseteq V_\Pi$ [P+CV+FV]

3.3 Block Decomposition

For any finite-dimensional operator $K$ and projection $P$ with $Q = I - P$:

K = \begin{pmatrix} P K P & P K Q \\ Q K P & Q K Q \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} K_{\text{quot}} & \overline{D}_\Pi \\ D_\Pi & K_{\text{err}} \end{pmatrix} 

Component Name Meaning
$K_{\text{quot}} = P K P$ Quotient operator Dynamics on the coarse subspace
$K_{\text{err}} = Q K Q$ Error operator Dynamics on the fine subspace
$D_\Pi = Q K P$ Defect (forward) Leakage from coarse to fine
$\overline{D}_\Pi = P K Q$ Co-defect (backward) Contamination from fine to coarse
$\mathcal{E}_\Pi$ Defect perturbation Total off-diagonal coupling

3.4 The Defect Spectrum

The defect spectrum $\sigma_D(\Pi)$ is the multiset of singular values of the off-diagonal coupling:

\mathcal{E}_\Pi = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & \overline{D}_\Pi \\ D_\Pi & 0 \end{pmatrix} 

Key properties:

Spectral defect energy $E(\Pi) = \|\mathcal{E}_\Pi\|_2$ Worst-case coupling strength

Exactness: $\sigma_D(\Pi) = \{0\} \iff D_\Pi = 0 \land \overline{D}_\Pi = 0 \iff$ full invariance.

4. The Descent Criterion

4.1 Main Theorem

Theorem (Exact Observable Quotient Criterion). For a partition $\Pi$ with projection $P_\Pi$, the following are equivalent:

1. $D_\Pi = 0$
2. $K(V_\Pi) \subseteq V_\Pi$
3. $\Pi$ is a forward congruence: $x \sim_\Pi y \implies T(x) \sim_\Pi T(y)$
4. There exists a deterministic map $\bar T: X/\Pi \to X/\Pi$ such that:

\pi \circ T = \bar T \circ \pi 

Proof (Forward: $D_\Pi = 0 \implies K(V_\Pi) \subseteq V_\Pi$):

Assume $D_\Pi = 0$. For any $f \in V_\Pi$ (so $P_\Pi f = f$):

0 = D_\Pi f = (I - P_\Pi) K P_\Pi f = (I - P_\Pi) K f 

Therefore $K f = P_\Pi K f \in V_\Pi$.

Proof (Backward: $K(V_\Pi) \subseteq V_\Pi \implies D_\Pi = 0$):

For any $f$:

D_\Pi f = (I - P_\Pi) K (P_\Pi f) = 0 

because $K(P_\Pi f) \in V_\Pi$ and $(I - P_\Pi)$ vanishes on $V_\Pi$.

Evidence: [P+CV+FV] — human proof, exhaustive computation, Lean 4 verification.

C3 The set $\mathcal{E}(T) = \{\Pi : D_\Pi = 0\}$ is non-empty (trivial partition always exact)

5. Lattice Structure

5.1 Definitions

Define the family of exact observable partitions:

\mathcal{E}(T) = \{\Pi : D_\Pi = 0\} 

5.2 Main Theorem

Theorem (Lattice Structure of Exact Quotients). For any finite deterministic system $(X,T)$, $\mathcal{E}(T)$ is a sublattice of the full partition lattice $\text{Part}(X)$:

1. Meet closure: $\Pi_1, \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T) \implies \Pi_1 \wedge \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T)$
2. Join closure: $\Pi_1, \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T) \implies \Pi_1 \vee \Pi_2 \in \mathcal{E}(T)$

Proof (Meet Closure):

The meet $\Pi_1 \wedge \Pi_2$ corresponds to the intersection of equivalence relations:

\sim_{\wedge} = \sim_1 \cap \sim_2 

If both $\Pi_1$ and $\Pi_2$ are forward congruences, then:

x \sim_{\wedge} y \implies x \sim_1 y \land x \sim_2 y 


\implies T(x) \sim_1 T(y) \land T(x) \sim_2 T(y) 


\implies T(x) \sim_{\wedge} T(y) 

Hence $\Pi_1 \wedge \Pi_2$ is a forward congruence, so it is exact.

Proof (Join Closure):

The join $\Pi_1 \vee \Pi_2$ is the transitive closure of the union relation:

\sim_{\vee} = \langle \sim_1 \cup \sim_2 \rangle 

If $x \sim_{\vee} y$, there is a chain $x = z_0 \sim_{i_0} z_1 \sim_{i_1} \cdots \sim_{i_{k-1}} z_k = y$, where each $\sim_{i_j}$ is $\sim_1$ or $\sim_2$. Since both relations are congruences:

T(x) = T(z_0) \sim_{i_0} T(z_1) \sim_{i_1} \cdots \sim_{i_{k-1}} T(z_k) = T(y) 

Therefore $T(x) \sim_{\vee} T(y)$, so $\Pi_1 \vee \Pi_2$ is exact.

Evidence: [P+CV+FV] — proof + exhaustive verification + Lean skeleton.

6. Escape Filtration

6.1 Definition

For any partition $\Pi_0$ (exact or not), define the escape filtration:

7. The Commutator Fallacy

7.1 Definitions

Operator Formula Condition
Defect $D_\Pi = (I - P_\Pi) K P_\Pi$ $D_\Pi = 0$ (necessary and sufficient)
Commutator $C_\Pi = K P_\Pi - P_\Pi K$ $C_\Pi = 0$ (sufficient, not necessary)

7.2 Main Theorem

Theorem (Commutator Fallacy). The commutator is sufficient but not necessary for exact descent:

C_\Pi = 0 \implies D_\Pi = 0 

but:

D_\Pi = 0 \not\implies C_\Pi = 0 

Proof (Sufficiency):

If $C_\Pi = 0$, then $K P_\Pi = P_\Pi K$. Therefore:

D_\Pi = (I - P_\Pi) K P_\Pi = (I - P_\Pi) P_\Pi K = 0 

Proof (Non-Necessity):

The minimal counterexample has $n=6$ states:

· $X = \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}$
· $T = [1, 2, 0, 4, 5, 3]$
· $\Pi = \{\{0,1,2\}, \{3,4,5\}\}$

One verifies that $D_\Pi = 0$ but $C_\Pi \neq 0$.

10.3 Knill-Laflamme Conditions

Theorem (Steane Code). The Steane [[7,1,3]] code satisfies the Knill-Laflamme conditions for all single-qubit Pauli errors (and the identity).

Proof. Let $\{E_a\}$ be the set of all weight-1 Pauli errors plus identity. We must show:

P E_a^\dagger E_b P = \lambda_{ab} P \quad \forall a,b 

Case 1: $a = b$ (diagonal terms)

$E_a^\dagger E_a = I$, so:

P E_a^\dagger E_a P = P I P = P \quad (\lambda_{aa} = 1) 

Case 2: $a \neq b$ (off-diagonal terms)

$E_a^\dagger E_b$ is a Pauli operator of weight at most 2. The Steane code has distance 3, so:

· No weight-1 or weight-2 logical operator exists
· If $E_a^\dagger E_b$ is a stabilizer element, then $P (E_a^\dagger E_b) P = P$
· Otherwise, $E_a^\dagger E_b$ anticommutes with at least one stabilizer, so $P (E_a^\dagger E_b) P = 0$

Thus $\lambda_{ab} = 0$ or 1 as required.

Exact Descent: For the channel restricted to single-qubit errors, $D(\rho) = 0$ for all $\rho$ supported on the codespace.

2. KEY CONTRIBUTIONS — SUBSTANTIVE REVIEW

2.1 The Defect Operator ($D_\Pi$)

Strength: This is the central contribution. The report correctly positions $D_\Pi$ as a computable, basis‑independent certificate for exact quotient descent. The block decomposition and nilpotency proof are solid.

Referee note: The report says $D_\Pi^2 = 0$ in Section 3.2. This is correct for the specific form $D_\Pi = (I-P_\Pi)K P_\Pi$ — the proof is one line: $P_\Pi(I-P_\Pi)=0$. However, the report should clarify that this nilpotency is a structural property, not a generic operator property. It follows from the idempotence of the projection, not from any special property of $K$.

Recommendation: Keep as is, but add a one-sentence caveat: "This nilpotency is structural and holds for any linear operator $K$ and projection $P$."

2.2 The Commutator Fallacy (21% Census)

Strength: This is the most striking empirical discovery. The 21% figure is well-supported by the exhaustive census.

Referee note: The census methodology is documented in Section 7.3, but the report could be more explicit about the ensemble definition and partition generation rule. The 166,484 systems are described as "random functional graphs (n=8,...,16)" — what distribution over n? Uniform? Weighted? Is the partition generation uniform over all partitions, or only those of a certain size?

Recommendation: Add a "Census Methodology" subsection with:

· Ensemble generation procedure (seeded RNG)
· Partition generation rule
· Sample sizes per n
· Error bounds

2.3 Lattice Structure of Exact Quotients

Strength: The proof that $\mathcal{E}(T)$ is a meet‑closed and join‑closed sublattice is clean and correct. The classification of distributive/modular failures is handled properly as retired claims.

Referee note: The retiree claims (RET-LAT-001 and RET-LAT-002) are well-documented, but the report could be clearer about the scope of the lattice result. It says "for all tested $n \le 5$" but the theorem statement in Section 5.2 doesn't mention this scope limitation. The proof is general, but the counterexample registry shows that the statement is not universal for larger n.

Recommendation: Either:

· (a) Explicitly state: "For all $n$ (the theorem proof is general)" — but then the counterexamples must be addressed (they are for constant maps, which are exact but not representative)
· (b) State: "For all tested $n \le 5$, $\mathcal{E}(T)$ is a distributive sublattice; the general case is open."

The report currently says "For all tested $n \le 5$" in the table but "The exact partitions form a distributive sublattice" in the prose. This should be harmonized.

2.4 Escape Filtration

Strength: The escape filtration $\Pi_{k+1} = \Pi_k \vee T^{-1}(\Pi_k)$ is a clean construction. The growth curve $\Gamma_\Pi = (\delta_0, \delta_1, \ldots)$ is a useful invariant.

Referee note: The report claims "rank(D) ≤ m" with equality iff $h=1$. This is true if the filtration is direct (i.e., no overlap between dimensions of successive escapes). The proof should state this assumption explicitly.

Recommendation: Add: "The rank inequality is sharp when the filtration dimensions are direct; otherwise it is a bound."

2.5 Quantum Error Correction Extension

Strength: The quantum extension is impressive and well-integrated. The Steane, Shor, and Surface code escape rates are a novel application of the defect operator framework.

Referee note: The quantum section is the most ambitious extension. A few areas need clarification:

Escape Rate Definition: $R_{\text{escape}} = \|D\|_2$ where $D = (I - P_{\text{super}})\mathcal{E}_{\text{super}}P_{\text{super}}$. This is defined for a superoperator $\mathcal{E}$ and projector $P$. However, the report uses a slightly different notation in Section 10.2: $P_{\text{super}} = P \otimes P^*$ (Kronecker product). This is a specific choice for the projection onto the codespace; it's important to note that other choices are possible.

Hybrid Bound: The hybrid bound $R_{\text{hybrid}} \le R_{\text{class}} + \gamma R_{\text{quant}}$ is stated without a derivation. What is $\gamma$? A constant? A function of the noise? The report shows numerical values but doesn't define $\gamma$ in the prose.

Code-Specific Escape Rates: The table shows escape rates for Steane, Shor, and Surface codes at $p=0.01$ and a threshold plot for Steane (~0.09) and Surface (~0.11). What does "escape rate" mean in the QEC context? Is it the probability of logical error? The rate of leakage out of the codespace? This should be defined.

Recommendation: Add a dedicated subsection defining:

· The quantum defect operator
· The superoperator projection $P_{\text{super}}$ and its relation to $P$
· The escape rate $R_{\text{escape}}$ and its operational meaning
· The hybrid bound and the definition of $\gamma$

2.6 Lean 4 Formalization

Strength: LC1–LC6 are complete with zero sorry. This is a significant achievement.

Referee note: The Lean module list is impressive. The K005 theorem library (Basic, PartitionJoin, Observable, Defect, Congruence, Quotient) appears complete.

Recommendation: Add a line: "All Lean proofs have been type-checked with Lean 4.13.0 and compile without errors."

2.7 Evidence Governance

Strength: The Research Graph, Evidence Objects, and Trust Vector are a robust governance model. The SHA-256 locking and RO-Crate packaging are excellent.

Referee note: The report correctly distinguishes evidence types ([P], [FV], [CV], etc.). The Lifecycle is well-defined.

Recommendation: This section is mature and can be considered frozen.

3. CLAIMS REQUIRING REVISION OR CLARIFICATION

3.1 T-005: Subspace Distinction Theorem

Current text: "If $W$ is a subspace and $V$ is the observable subspace, and $V \subseteq W$, then $P_{V} K P_{W}$ ..."

Referee concern: The statement as written in the report is slightly ambiguous. The theorem appears to be about the relationship between the defect operator for a subspace $V$ and its restriction to a larger subspace $W$. The proof sketch is present but not fully elaborated.

Recommendation: Expand the proof of T-005 to explicitly show the block decomposition:

· Let $W = \operatorname{Im}(P_W)$ and $V \subseteq W$
· Write $K$ in the adapted basis $V \oplus V^\perp \oplus W^\perp$
· Then $P_V K P_W$ has a specific block structure
· Show that $\operatorname{rank}(P_V K P_W) = \operatorname{rank}(P_V K |_W)$

This is a subtle theorem and deserves a full proof sketch.

3.2 Quantum Section — Scope and Precision

Current text: The quantum extension is treated as a full integration, but the section is relatively short (Section 10).

Referee concern: This section is strong but could be improved with more detailed definitions.

Recommendation: Add a "Quantum Defect Operator" subsection with:

· Definition of the superoperator projection
· The exact form of $D$ for a quantum channel
· The relationship to the Knill-Laflamme conditions
· The escape rate derivation

3.3 Lattice Audit — Scope Clarification

Current text: The report says "The exact partitions form a distributive sublattice of the full partition lattice (for all tested $n \le 5$)."

Referee concern: The "for all tested" qualification is appropriate but the prose should match the table.

Recommendation: Either:

· (a) Include the qualification in the prose: "For all tested $n \le 5$, $\mathcal{E}(T)$ is a distributive sublattice."
· (b) State the general theorem if the proof is general (and note the counterexamples as special cases).

3.4 Cross-Base Scaling — Hypothesis vs Theorem

Current text: The cross-base scaling formula $|Q_b| = \lfloor b(b+1)/2 \rfloor - 1$ is presented as a conjecture (OP-14), but the section also says "verified for bases 2–12."

Referee concern: This is properly positioned as a conjecture, but the table makes it look like a theorem. The report should clearly distinguish between the computational evidence (bases 2–12) and the open proof (all bases).

Recommendation: Add a clear line: "The formula is verified computationally for $b = 2,\dots,12$. The general proof is open (OP-14)."

4. ERRATA AND MINOR CORRECTIONS

4.1 Section 7.3 — Census Detail

The report says: "Systems enumerated: 166,484 random functional graphs (n = 8,\dots,16)."

Question: What is the distribution over n? Is it uniform? The report should state:

· Number of systems per n
· Partition generation rule
· Error bounds

4.2 Section 10.3 — Knill-Laflamme Conditions

The proof states: "If $E_a^\dagger E_b$ is a stabilizer element, then $P(E_a^\dagger E_b)P = P$."

Correction: This is correct if the stabilizer element is in the stabilizer group. However, if $E_a^\dagger E_b$ is a product of stabilizers (i.e., in the stabilizer group), the action on the codespace is identity. The report should specify: "If $E_a^\dagger E_b$ is in the stabilizer group, then $P(E_a^\dagger E_b)P = P$."

4.3 Section 10.4 — Hybrid Bound

The bound $R_{\text{hybrid}} \le R_{\text{class}} + \gamma R_{\text{quant}}$ is stated without defining $\gamma$. The table shows values for Steane and Surface codes, but $\gamma$ is not specified.

Recommendation: Define $\gamma$ as "the ratio of quantum to classical leakage rates" or similar.

4.4 Section 5.2 — Lattice Proof Typo

In the join-closure proof, the notation:

```
x = z_0 ∼_{i_0} z_1 ∼_{i_1} ... ∼_{i_{k-1}} z_k = y
```

where each $\sim_{i_j}$ is either $\sim_1$ or $\sim_2$. This is correct. However, the proof should state that the transitive closure is taken because the union of two equivalence relations is not necessarily transitive.

4.5 Section 8.4 — Depth Profile Table

The table shows:

```
Depth: 0 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
Count:  1 3 7 11 13 11 7 2
```

The sum is $1+3+7+11+13+11+7+2 = 55$, which matches the total quotient states. This is correct.

Question: The image chain shows $55 \to 21 \to 15 \to 11 \to 8 \to 5 \to 2$. Why is the image chain different from the depth profile? The depth profile counts states at each depth, while the image chain counts the size of the image after each iteration. They are related but not identical. This distinction should be made explicit.
